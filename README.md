## 🚫 The 6 Non-Negotiable Copywriting Rules

Paste this block at the top of any outreach prompt. These rules do most of the work.

```
You are writing first-touch outreach. These rules are non-negotiable — a message that breaks them is a failed message, rewrite it:

C-1 — NO FLUFF. Never open with "I hope this finds you well", "In today's fast-paced world",
      "I wanted to reach out", or "I'm writing to introduce". Start with the HOOK.
C-2 — 4-LINE HOOK STRUCTURE: Hook (specific researched observation) → Problem (their exact pain,
      quantified) → Value (one plain sentence, zero jargon) → Soft CTA (ask for interest, NOT a meeting).
C-3 — LENGTH: WhatsApp/DM under 75 words. Cold email body under 100 words. Subject under 8 words.
C-4 — TONE: Peer-to-peer. Busy exec to busy exec. Short sentences. Plain words. No flattery.
C-5 — REAL DETAILS ONLY. Never invent placeholder names, phones, or [brackets] in the final copy.
C-6 — BANNED WORDS: "cutting-edge", "innovative", "streamline", "leverage", "synergy",
      "game-changer", "best-in-class". If you write one, you failed.
```

## 📨 Cold Email — the 5-line framework

```
Write a cold email using this exact structure. Apply the 6 copywriting rules above.

Subject: [4–7 words, see subject-line formulas]
[No greeting. Open straight on the hook.]

Line 1 — HOOK: A specific, researched observation about [PROSPECT COMPANY]'s industry or a trigger event.
Line 2 — PROBLEM: The exact pain [their sector] faces, quantified. ("Most [sector] owners lose X hours/week on Y.")
Line 3 — VALUE: What we do, in one plain sentence. No jargon.
Line 4 — PROOF: One result or credibility signal. ("Helped [N] businesses [outcome] in [timeframe].")
Line 5 — SOFT CTA: One low-commitment ask. "Worth a quick look?" / "Open to seeing a breakdown?"

Signature: [Real name, company, phone, email]

CONTEXT:
- My product: [what you sell, one line]
- Target: [who you're writing to — role + industry]
- Proof point: [one real result or number]
```

## ✉️ Subject lines that get opened

Pick the formula that fits your objective. **Rules: under 8 words, no ALL CAPS, never "Quick Question" / "Following Up" / "Touching Base" / fake "Re:".**

| Formula | Template |
|---|---|
| Curiosity gap | `Why [sector] companies are switching from [old way]` |
| Number-led | `3 things slowing down your [process]` |
| Direct question | `Still dealing with [specific pain]?` |
| Direct observation | `Noticed [specific thing] about [company]` |
| FOMO | `What top [sector] businesses do differently` |
| Outcome promise | `How [peer] cut [cost/time] by [X]%` |

> Pair the subject with a **pre-header (90–110 chars)** that *extends* the story — never repeats the subject. Together they're your inbox hook.

## 🔁 The follow-up sequence

The first email captures ~58% of replies. The rest come from following up *with new value* — never "just checking in."

```
Day 0  — First touch (the 5-line framework above)
Day 3  — Add ONE new value-add: an industry stat, a tip, or a 1-line case study. Never "following up."
Day 7  — Change the angle. New pain point, or a yes/no question they can answer in 2 seconds.
Day 14 — The break-up email: "Looks like the timing isn't right — I'll close this off.
         If anything changes, just reply." (This consistently triggers the most replies.)
Day 28 — Re-engage with a fresh hook if they went cold after a positive signal.
```

**Benchmarks to aim for (B2B cold):** open >25% good / >40% excellent · reply >5% good / >12% excellent · unsubscribe <0.5% healthy.

## 📣 Email marketing — campaign structure

For nurturing a list (not 1:1 cold). Segment first — segmented campaigns get dramatically more clicks than blasts.

```
EMAIL 1 (Welcome):  Who you are, the problem you solve, ONE insight. No hard sell.
EMAIL 2 (Educate):  Deep-dive the #1 pain. Real value. Soft mention of how you solve it.
EMAIL 3 (Proof):    Case study / testimonial. Specific numbers. Let results sell.
EMAIL 4 (Objection): Address the top 2 objections head-on. Defuse them proactively.
EMAIL 5 (Offer):    One clear, time-relevant offer. Single CTA. Genuine urgency only.
EMAIL 6 (Re-engage): For non-openers — new subject, shorter, different angle.
```

**Send-time:** Tue/Wed/Thu, 8–10 AM or 1–2 PM. Avoid Monday AM and Friday PM.

## 📱 Social media — platform-native rules

The #1 mistake: writing one post and pasting it everywhere. Each platform has its own physics. **Tell your AI the platform, and enforce its rules:**

```
X (Twitter):  Under 280 chars total. 1 bold statement → 1 proof point → 1 takeaway. 1–2 hashtags max.
LinkedIn:     Heavy whitespace, one idea per line. Broetry structure (below). End with a question. 3–5 hashtags at the bottom.
Facebook:     50–150 words. Warm, community tone. Local problem → solution → friendly CTA. 2–4 emojis.
Instagram:    First line must hook before "...more". 5–15 niche hashtags (put them in first comment). Describe the visual.
TikTok/Shorts: Script format. Pattern interrupt in first 3 seconds. 30–60 sec. Caption everything (85% watch on mute).

RULE: If I ask for an X post and you give me a LinkedIn post, that's a failure.
```

## ✍️ The LinkedIn "broetry" structure

The single highest-engagement organic format on LinkedIn:

```
Line 1 (HOOK):   Bold claim / surprising stat / contrarian take. Must stop the scroll.
                 Do NOT start with "I", "We", or your company name.
[blank line]     ← forces the "see more" click = dwell-time boost
3–5 short paras  one or two sentences each. Whitespace is the point.
[blank line]
3–5 punchy lines bullets or numbered insights. One idea per line.
[blank line]
Final line:      ONE question to drive comments — "What would you add?"
3–5 hashtags at the very bottom. No more.
```

**Why it works:** LinkedIn rewards early comments (first 60 min), dwell time, and comment-weight over likes. The structure is engineered to trigger all three.

## 🪝 Hooks that stop the scroll

```
Contrarian:     "Most [topic] advice is wrong. Here's what actually works:"
Number-led:     "I studied 50 [sector] businesses. Here's what separated the top 10%:"
Personal story: "6 months ago I [struggled]. Today [outcome]. Here's what changed:"
Pattern break:  "[Conventional wisdom]. Everyone says this. It's wrong."
Curiosity:      "The #1 mistake [audience] make (that no one talks about):"
Video/Short:    "Stop doing this if you're a [role]" / "[N] things [audience] get wrong about [topic]"
```

## 🎬 Short-video script structure

```
0–3s:   PATTERN INTERRUPT — bold text + the most curious/shocking line. This is your whole bet.
3–10s:  PROMISE — what they'll learn. "Here's why [thing] is wrong."
10–40s: DELIVERY — one idea per cut, no dead air, text overlay on every key point.
40–55s: VALUE REVEAL — the specific insight or result.
55–60s: CTA — "Comment [word] and I'll DM you the template."
```
Always vertical 9:16. Caption everything. Natural window light beats a ring light.
